Wireless earbuds are one of the most crowded product categories in consumer tech. Every brand has a flagship, every flagship claims to be best-in-class, and the spec sheets all start to blur together. We cut through it.

Over the past two months, we tested **14 pairs of wireless earbuds** across noise cancellation, sound quality, call performance, comfort, and battery life. These are the ones worth your money in 2025.

## Quick Picks

| Pick | Price | Best For |
|------|-------|----------|
| Sony WF-1000XM5 | ~$279 | Best overall |
| AirPods Pro 2 | ~$249 | iPhone users |
| Jabra Elite 10 | ~$249 | Android / multipoint |
| Soundcore Liberty 4 NC | ~$79 | Budget shoppers |

---

## 1. Sony WF-1000XM5 — Best Overall

The WF-1000XM5 improves on an already excellent predecessor with a new 8.4mm driver, a slimmer case, and a redesigned stem that fits more ear shapes comfortably. The noise cancellation is still the benchmark — it remains the best single metric we've measured across any earbud.

Sound quality is warm and detailed, with a slight low-end emphasis that's flattering for most genres without being muddy. LDAC support (on Android) pushes the audio ceiling noticeably higher if you have hi-res content to feed it.

Where Sony still falls short: the touch controls are fiddly, the EQ app (Sound Connect) is functional but not great, and the call quality — while improved — still trails Jabra.

**Battery:** 8 hours (earbuds) + 16 hours (case). Fast charging delivers 1 hour of playback in 5 minutes.

---

## 2. Apple AirPods Pro 2 — Best for iPhone Users

Don't use an iPhone? Stop reading here. Use an iPhone? The AirPods Pro 2 are almost certainly the right answer.

The H2 chip enables features that competitors still can't match: **Adaptive Audio** blends ANC and transparency modes dynamically based on your environment. Conversation Awareness drops volume and activates transparency automatically when you start talking. Personalized Spatial Audio actually works. The integration is just deeper than anything else on the market.

The ANC itself is excellent — second only to the Sony XM5. Battery life is solid at 6 hours per charge (30 hours with the case). And the new USB-C case finally kills the Lightning port.

The one real downside: if you're not in the Apple ecosystem, none of the best features work. Don't buy these for an Android phone.

---

## 3. Jabra Elite 10 — Best for Android / Power Users

Jabra doesn't get enough credit. The Elite 10 has the best **call quality** in this roundup by a meaningful margin — the 6-mic array and AI call processing make a noticeable difference in noisy environments. If you take calls all day, this is the pair.

Multipoint Bluetooth (connect to two devices at once) is implemented better here than anywhere else we tested. Switching from laptop to phone mid-call is actually seamless rather than a 10-second ritual.

The ANC is good, not great — it handles background hum well but lets in more ambient conversation noise than the Sony or Apple offerings. Sound quality is neutral and accurate, with a slightly clinical tuning that audiophiles will appreciate and casual listeners might find lacking warmth.

**Battery:** 6 hours + 24 hours case. Qi wireless charging supported.

---

## 4. Soundcore Liberty 4 NC — Best Budget Pick

At around $79, the Liberty 4 NC is genuinely remarkable. The ANC is real — not performative — and actually makes a difference on a commute or in an open-plan office. Sound quality is balanced and pleasant, and the 10mm driver delivers more bass extension than you'd expect at this price.

Yes, the build feels plasticky compared to the $250 options. Yes, the companion app is cluttered. And the call quality is mediocre. But if your budget is under $100, this is the pair to get.

---

## How to Choose: Buying Guide

**Noise cancellation priority?** Sony WF-1000XM5, full stop. Nothing else in this roundup matches it.

**Deep in the Apple ecosystem?** AirPods Pro 2. The ecosystem integration genuinely earns the premium.

**Android power user or call quality matters?** Jabra Elite 10.

**Budget under $100?** Soundcore Liberty 4 NC. Don't let anyone sell you on spending more than you need to.

**Fit issues?** All four of these ship with multiple ear tip sizes. If you've historically struggled with earbud fit, look for models with foam tips (Sony includes them; the others use silicone by default).
